Component-model study · stage (c)

Four conventions, translated into the no-build idiom.

What shadcn/ui actually standardizes — anatomy, variants, tokens-as-contract, composition — extracted as structure and translated for a system that ships hand-authored HTML/CSS/JSX with no bundler. shadcn is a distribution system that amplifies design decisions; it does not create them. The decisions here already exist — hairlines, one cyan, ink/paper, mono-caps, the --tk-* contract — so the study extracts shadcn's four structural conventions, not its software. The translations below are recorded as ADR-0011 and are what the component library (stage d, Component Gap Analysis.html) builds against.

Method, sources & scope

Sources (fresh top-3, per the stage-opening rule): the canonical anatomy deep-dive (manupa.dev, "The anatomy of shadcn/ui") — the two-layer split of headless structure/behavior vs. style, with style itself split into structural and visual properties; the official theming and CVA documentation — semantic background/foreground token pairs, named variant axes, cn() merge-order, and the Tailwind-v4-era refresh (data-slot on every primitive, forwardRef removed); and 2026 production-structure guidance — shadcn as a component distribution system that assumes design intent, with a layered tree (raw primitives → modified primitives → product blocks) and tokens defined once, consumed everywhere.

The strategic read: the BDS already owns what shadcn requires but cannot supply — the design intent. The --tk-og-* generator (ADR-0010) was the explicit rehearsal: tokens carry the design decisions, the component computes its own geometry, and retheming costs zero component edits. Stage (d) applies the same boundary to fifty components.

Out of scope here: building any component (stage d), reprioritizing the Gap Analysis, and adding tokens to tokens/palette.js — the §03 gap rows are proposals; each addition lands as its own change under ADR-0002 (single source) and ADR-0003 (versioning intent) discipline.
